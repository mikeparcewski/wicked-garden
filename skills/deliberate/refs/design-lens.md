# Design Lens

Additional questions for the five lenses when the work involves UX, UI, or design.

## Lens 1 Additions: Is This Real?

- Is this a usability issue or a learning curve? Would onboarding solve it?
- Is the user's mental model wrong, or is the design misleading?
- How many users are affected vs. how many adapted without issue?

## Lens 2 Additions: What's Actually Going On?

- Is the problem in this screen, or in the flow that led here?
- Is the information hierarchy wrong? (important things not prominent)
- Is the issue visual (styling) or structural (wrong component/pattern)?

## Lens 3 Additions: What Else Can We Fix?

- Do other screens have the same usability issue?
- Is the design system missing a component this screen needs?
- Are similar interactions inconsistent across the product?

## Lens 4 Additions: Should We Rethink?

- Would removing a step eliminate the confusion entirely?
- Would progressive disclosure solve the overwhelm?
- Should this be a different interaction pattern altogether?

## Lens 5 Additions: Better Way?

- Can we use a standard platform pattern users already know?
- Can we show instead of tell? (preview, inline help, smart defaults)
- Can we remove options instead of adding explanations?
